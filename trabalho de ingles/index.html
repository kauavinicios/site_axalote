Axolotl

In recent years, many hobbyists have acquired a very friendly pet: the Axolotl, a fully aquatic salamander that is part of the same class that includes toads and frogs. These animals are originally from Mexico and can reach up to about 30 cm, are usually black or brownish, with white or albino variants (more common in captive species) and are predators of molluscs, small fish, insect larvae and crustaceans.

Axolotl: Extinction risk!

This amphibian lives in risk extinction because of the action of man.

Acording to a brazilian newsletter Super Abril (2016), “The amphibian population, although thought to be 10 years old, does not know exactly how many of them exist in the world. are operating with drought and water quality in their habitat. In addition, some non-native fish were inserted into the region's food chain, the Axolotl becomes prey.”.
Furthermore, there is still the illegal and uncontrolled sale of this species in many countries such as Brazil, therefore they are protected by specific bodies and according to the O Eco (2009), “for the importation and commercialization of these animals to be carried out, a license issued by IBAMA is required, in addition to registering the buyer and the trader in the National Wildlife Management System”.

Conservation and Reactivation of the Chinampera Zone of Xochimilco

The project was financed by the Federal Heritage Cities Program, through the management of the Municipality of Xochimilco, is part of a comprehensive strategy that, for little more than 15 years, is carrying out the Laboratory of Ecological Restoration from the Institute of Biology (IBUNAM) of the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), in close coordination with chinamperos and producers of Xochimilco with objective of conserving and protecting the cultural and ecological heritage of the chinampera zone of Xochimilco, a site recognized by UNESCO as a world heritage site.
